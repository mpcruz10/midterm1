*RuPaul's Drag Race*, which premiered in 2009, is a reality competition show where drag queens compete in various challenges to win the title of America's Next Drag Superstar. Hosted by RuPaul, the show celebrates drag culture, fashion, performance, and self-expression, becoming a groundbreaking platform for LGBTQ+ visibility. With its unique blend of humor, heart, and fierce competition, *Drag Race* has had a profound impact on pop culture, bringing drag into the mainstream and inspiring a global fanbase. It has sparked conversations around identity, gender, and creativity while influencing fashion, music, and social media. The show’s success has led to multiple seasons, spin-offs, and international versions, helping to shape the modern landscape of entertainment and making RuPaul a household name and a trailblazer for queer representation.

Which television channel premiered RuPaul's Drag Race?

RuPaul's Drag Race originally premiered on Logo TV in 2009. The show has since expanded to various platforms, including VH1, which began airing the show in 2017. Logo TV, a network known for its focus on LGBTQ+ content, was the original home for Drag Race, where it first gained widespread popularity before transitioning to VH1, and finally to MTV for a larger audience.
